THINGS TO KEEP IN MIND

Learn how to listen

It is critical that you improve your listening abilities. Keep your focus and concentration on what the speaker is saying rather than anticipating what you'll say and tuning out the speaker. You must listen to what others are saying if you want to gain a deeper understanding of them. As a result, be a good listener. This is not only respectful, but it is also crucial for the understanding and being understood process.

Think first, then speak

Begin by considering what you're going to say - well before you actually say it. Use the breathing in and out technique if this is tough. While you think how you want to frame your statements, take one or two deep breaths (without being too noticeable). What is the point of your meeting? Do you need to inform, obtain information, seek aid, sympathize, encourage, or offer advice? Your message will most likely be better heard and comprehended if you know why you need to say something.

Mean what you say

When others speak, most people have an intuitive ability to recognize phoniness. Your words are merely a component of the overall process of communication. Emotion, conviction, or a gap between what's being said and what the speaker intends or believes are also conveyed through tone, body language, emphasis on words or lack thereof, facial expressions, breathing, flushing, sweating, and other physical indications. You should make it a point to express the truth in accordance with your beliefs and the ideals you hold dear.
